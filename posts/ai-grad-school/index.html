Should you go to grad school for AI?

You’re interested in artificial intelligence. You’ve seen some of the awesome state of the art things that can be done with machine learning. So you want a piece of the action.

Grad school is expensive. How expensive will depend a lot on where you go and whether you get financial assistance (e.g. you may be able to TA). But one thing is absolutely clear: you are the customer. You’re probably going to be paying 10s of thousands for a degree, and that doesn’t take into account the salary you would have made had you not gone to school.

Well, here are some reasons you should not go to grad school:

  • You want to learn about artificial intelligence. Artificial intelligence is a very broad topic. Your Intro to AI course is going to be a survey of the field, which means you may end up learning about propositional logic and knowledge bases for a whole quarter/semester. If that’s what you’re interested in, more power to you.
  • You want to learn more about machine learning. Again, this will depend on the class and instructor. If you’re interested in state of the art stuff, make sure you’re not stuck building a neural network from scratch instead.
  • You can’t learn the basics on your own. Not all professors care about teaching. And they may not have to care. And once you pay tuition, they don’t even have to pretend to care. For “Intro to X” courses, the vast majority of content can be found online for free, and taught by people who are paid based on their ability to explain content.
